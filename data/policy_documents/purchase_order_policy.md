# Purchase Order Policy

**Document ID:** POL-PO-002  
**Version:** 3.1  
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2024  
**Owner:** Procurement Operations Team  
**Review Cycle:** Annual

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy defines the rules, approval thresholds, and processes governing the creation, issuance, modification, and closure of purchase orders (POs) within the organization.

## 2. Scope

Applies to all departments issuing purchase orders for goods, services, or capital expenditures. Personal reimbursements and petty cash transactions are excluded.

## 3. Purchase Order Thresholds and Approvals

| PO Value | Approver Required |
|----------|------------------|
| < $1,000 | Department Manager |
| $1,000 – $10,000 | Senior Manager or Director |
| $10,001 – $50,000 | VP of Operations |
| $50,001 – $250,000 | CFO |
| > $250,000 | CFO + CEO or Board Resolution |

Approval must be documented in the ERP system. Email approvals are not accepted unless routed through the formal exception process.

## 4. PO Creation Requirements

Every purchase order must include:

1. **Approved Supplier ID** — Must reference the Approved Supplier Registry
2. **Itemized Description** — Clear description of goods or services, including quantity and unit of measure
3. **UNSPSC Code** — Required for spend classification and analytics
4. **Delivery Date** — Expected delivery or service completion date
5. **Delivery Address** — Physical or digital delivery location
6. **Cost Center / Project Code** — For financial allocation
7. **Authorized Budget Reference** — Link to approved budget line

Incomplete POs will be returned to the requester within 2 business days.

## 5. Three-Way Match Process

All goods-based purchase orders require three-way match before invoice payment:

1. **Purchase Order** — Approved PO in system
2. **Goods Receipt** — Confirmed delivery in ERP
3. **Invoice** — Supplier invoice within 5% tolerance of PO amount

Discrepancies exceeding 5% require a Purchase Order Amendment before payment is processed.

## 6. Payment Terms

Standard payment terms:

| Supplier Category | Standard Terms |
|------------------|----------------|
| Strategic suppliers | Net 45 days |
| Standard approved suppliers | Net 30 days |
| New suppliers (first 3 POs) | Net 15 days |
| Utilities and recurring services | Due on receipt |

Early payment discounts of 2% Net 10 may be taken when cash flow permits, with manager approval.

## 7. PO Amendments

Amendments exceeding 10% of the original PO value require re-approval at the same threshold level as the original approval. Amendments below 10% require written approval from the original approver.

## 8. Emergency Purchase Orders

Emergency POs may bypass standard approval when:
- Operational stoppage risk exists
- Alternative approved supplier is not available
- Regulatory or safety requirement demands immediate action

Emergency POs require:
- Verbal approval from VP of Operations
- Written ratification within 24 hours
- Post-hoc documentation submitted to Procurement within 5 business days

## 9. PO Cancellation

Purchase orders may be cancelled before goods receipt with approval from the original approver. If the supplier has begun work or prepared materials:
- Cancellation fees may apply per the vendor agreement
- Finance must be notified within 24 hours of cancellation decision

## 10. UNSPSC Spend Classification

All purchase orders must include a valid UNSPSC code at segment or family level minimum. Procurement Operations maintains the internal UNSPSC reference catalog. If the appropriate code cannot be determined, the requester must contact the Procurement Analyst for classification assistance.

## 11. Record Retention

Purchase order records must be retained for a minimum of **7 years** in accordance with financial record retention requirements. ERP system records constitute the system of record.
