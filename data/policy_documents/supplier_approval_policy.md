# Supplier Approval Policy

**Document ID:** POL-SUP-001  
**Version:** 2.4  
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2024  
**Owner:** Procurement Governance Team  
**Review Cycle:** Annual

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes the mandatory requirements and process for approving new suppliers and maintaining the approved supplier registry. All purchase orders issued by the organization must reference an approved supplier unless an emergency exception has been formally granted.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all procurement activities exceeding USD $1,000 per transaction or $5,000 annually with a single supplier. It covers direct material suppliers, service providers, and logistics vendors.

## 3. Required Documentation

All suppliers must submit the following documentation before being added to the Approved Supplier Registry:

### 3.1 Mandatory Documents

| Document | Description | Expiry |
|----------|-------------|--------|
| W-9 Form (US) or equivalent tax form | Tax identification verification | On change |
| Certificate of Insurance | General liability minimum $2M, Workers Comp | Annual |
| Signed Vendor Agreement | Legal terms and conditions acceptance | Per agreement |
| Bank Account Verification | For ACH payment setup | On change |

### 3.2 Industry-Specific Documents

Suppliers in the following categories must also submit:

- **Chemicals:** Safety Data Sheets (SDS/MSDS), EPA Compliance Certificate
- **Food and Beverage:** FDA Registration, FSMA Compliance Statement
- **Electronics:** RoHS/REACH compliance declaration
- **Construction/Engineering:** Contractor License, Bonding Certificate

## 4. Approval Process

### Step 1: Supplier Self-Registration
Suppliers complete the online Supplier Registration Portal with company information, capabilities, and document uploads.

### Step 2: Document Review
The Procurement Governance Team reviews submitted documents within **10 business days**. Incomplete submissions are returned with a documented list of missing items.

### Step 3: Risk Assessment
All new suppliers undergo a risk assessment scoring:

- **Low Risk (Score 70–100):** Expedited approval, no site visit required
- **Medium Risk (Score 40–69):** Standard approval, reference checks required
- **High Risk (Score 0–39):** Extended review, site visit or third-party audit may be required

### Step 4: Approval Decision
The Procurement Director approves suppliers with risk score ≥ 40. High-risk suppliers require VP of Operations approval.

### Step 5: Registry Addition
Approved suppliers are added to the ERP Approved Supplier Registry within **3 business days** of final approval.

## 5. Missing Documentation Policy

Suppliers with missing documentation must resolve outstanding items within **30 calendar days** of notification. 

- After 30 days: Supplier is flagged as **Conditional** — no new POs may be issued
- After 60 days without resolution: Supplier status set to **Suspended**
- After 90 days: Supplier may be removed from the registry

Procurement personnel may not issue purchase orders to Conditional or Suspended suppliers without written approval from the Procurement Director.

## 6. Annual Recertification

All approved suppliers must recertify annually. The Procurement team sends recertification requests 60 days before the anniversary date. Suppliers that do not complete recertification are automatically set to Conditional status.

## 7. Supplier Onboarding Communication

After final approval, the Procurement team sends a Welcome Letter including:
- Approved Supplier ID
- Assigned Procurement Contact
- Purchase Order Submission Process
- Payment Terms Reference

## 8. Exceptions

Emergency exceptions may be granted by the Procurement Director for operational continuity. All exceptions must be:
1. Documented in writing within 24 hours
2. Limited to a single transaction
3. Followed by standard onboarding initiation within 5 business days

## 9. Compliance and Audit

This policy is audited quarterly. Non-compliance incidents are reported to the VP of Finance and the Compliance Committee.
